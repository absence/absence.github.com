In philosophy, systems theory, science, and art, emergence is the way complex systems and patterns arise out of a multiplicity of relatively simple interactions. Emergence is central to the theories of integrative levels and of complex systems...

Incomplete Nature glossary:

Emergence: A term used to designate an apparently discontinuous transition from one mode of causal properties to another of a higher rank, typically associated with an increase in scale in which lower-order component interactions contribute global properties that appear irreducible to the lower-order interactions. The term has a long and diverse history, but throughout this history it has been used to describe the way that living and mental processes depend upon chemical and physical processes, yet exhibit collective properties not exhibited by non-living and non-mental processes, and in many cases appear to violate the ubiquitous tendencies exhibited by these component interactions.

This section:

  • Pre-Socratic Greece to 19th C: The whole is the sum of the parts
  • The best way to understand things is to break something down into its constituent parts
  • Therefore the quest has always been to try find the smallest part
  • Reductionism (AKA 'smallism') is introduced and various biases/issues are characterized
  • Current trend is towards complexity and interaction

Key words: Emergentism, Reductionism, Empedocles, Democritus, Atomism, Thomas Hobbes, Robert A. Wilson, Smallism, DNA, Stephen Hawking

Topics covered: History, Biochemistry, Philosophy

Emergentism was a response to another 19th century theory: Reductionism. p151

Reductionism: complicated phenomena may be be better understood by separating them into component parts. p152

The whole may be understood as the interaction of the parts. An extension to Atomism - keep dividing things until you get to the smallest possible bit. p152

But: you never reach the indivisible. There's no stopping it. p152

5 A HOUSE OF CARDS? - p164

This section:

  • Jaegwon Kim's critique of emergentist theories are described
  • Some parts may have a meronic validity, but even these have issues
  • There cannot be a change of thought without a change at the neuronal/biochemical level
  • If it's organized all the way down, and nothing above can happen without without something below having happened, then how can anything emerge?
  • So alternative ideas regarding emergence are needed - particularly ones that take time into consideration - a dynamic systems approach

Key words: Jaegwon Kim, Causal closure , Jerry Fodor, Supervenience, Donald Davidson, Part/whole, Mereology, Functionalism, Quantum physics, Quantum fields, Mark Bickhard,

Wikipedia definition:

Mereology treats parts and the wholes they form. Whereas set theory is founded on the membership relation between a set and its elements, mereology emphasizes the meronomic relation between entities, which from a set theoretic perspective is closer to that of inclusion between sets.

Topics covered: philosophy, quantum mechanics

Issue with emergence theories: possibility of double counting leading to infinite regression: An end result causing itself.

Jaegwon Kim challenges the popular assumptions of emergentists - forces us to think outside the box. p164

Kim's critique based on a core issue of emergence: supervenience

Donald Davidson defines: there cannot be two things that are physically identical but differ in some mental respects. Or an object cannot alter some mental respect without altering in some physical aspect. p165

Or there cannot be a change of thought without a change at the neuronal level. p165

If so, then every possible higher order property is dependent on a lower order property but then nothing can ever emerge. p165

No difference in the whole is possible without a difference in the parts. Therefore how could there be something in the whole that is not reducible to a combination of properties of the parts? p165

The part/whole study is critical and has pre-Socratic origins. See also mereology. p165

Kim uses mereology principle of defining parts and wholes so as to avoid double counting. p165

No matter how similar the function, form and multiple realizibility - there's always an observable difference. p166

Substance metaphysics does not support quantum physics. p167

Example: quantum physics: there no 'atoms'/ultimate small particle down there - just quantum fields. p166

At this level features of matter are statistical regularities. p167

Mereology only begins to succeed at a Newtonian level after statistical regularity of quantum fields have been taken into account. Remember this is just an example of failure, not itself leading towards a solution. p167

Mark Bickhard: to say that particles participate in organization, but are not themselves organized is pointless. Particle behavior is process-like and thus by definition organized. p187

If it's [turtles ;-] organized all the way down, and nothing above can happen without without something below having happened, then how can anything emerge?

Another issue is synchronicity. Higher order properties were not always present. Life and mind are just about brand new in the universe. Emergence is emergent. p168

Looking at emergence as an ongoing dynamic system helps, so do the many new type of exemplars of emergence as well as new tools. p168

20th century: two reasons emergence re-emerges: 1. organizations discovered within inorganic phenomena, 2. advent of computer simulations. p168

The underlying algorithms produce non-linear results, but they are recursive

Famous example: Edward Lorenz / 1960s: Lorenz attractor an example of deterministic chaos. 171
