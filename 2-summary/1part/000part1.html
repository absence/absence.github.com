Part 1 Summary ~ Chapters 1-5 ~ Outlining the Problem

Deacon summarizes Part 1 as follows:

I show that the conceptual riddles posed by absential phenomena have not been dealt with, despite claims that they have been overcome, but rather that they have been swept under the rug in various ingenious ways. By critiquing the history of efforts to explain them or explain them away, I argue that our various efforts have only served to insinuate these difficulties more cryptically into our current scientific and humanistic paradigms.

Chapters

  1. Absence

    Introduces the term 'absential'. Can what is not there matter? Can the ansential have physical, scientifically verifiable consequence?

    Why is the absential missing in science?

  2. (W)holes

    Introduces the adjective 'ententional'. How can something come from nothing?

  3. Homunculi

    Characterizes the term 'homunculis'. What is the mind in the mind/body problem?

  4. Golems

    Characterizes the term 'golem'. What is the body in the mind/body problem?

  5. Teleology

    Characterizes the terms 'teleology', 'teleonomy' and 'teleomatic'. Which came first - the mind or the body?

    Do living things disobey the laws of nature? What are the differences between human design, natural selection and happy accident? Are body parts teleological, teleonomic or teleomatic? How does purpose arise from stuff? How can things start with no purpose and yet end up with purpose>

  6. Emergence

    Characterizes the term 'emergence'. How and when did the mind/body problem discover itself?

    When did emergence emerge? How does stuff learn without a teacher? Are there correlations between size and emergence? Can reducing things to the simplest and smallest levels povide varifiable answers? Why can you reassemble a machine but not an animal?
