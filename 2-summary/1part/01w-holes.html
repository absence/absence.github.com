1 A stone's throw - p 18

Key words: causality, organization

This section provides descriptions of various aspects of the sepration of thought processes from phyical processes. It comsiders a vision (that really exists from a scientific point of view) of a future based on the past that informs the present.

Parable: a boy skips a stone across water. How can we explain what happened in a manner that takes the myriad thoughts and physical actions into account? p18-19

There is a huge discrepancy between what is spontaneouusly probable in the physical [inorganic] realm and the deviant [wildly improbable] improbable introdoced by organisms and minds. p20

Having fully detailed knowledge about the boy's physical state might only be able to predict the new few seconds of his activity.

A glimpse of the boy being shown how to skip a stone, could be vastly more predictive.

A shift from physical to mental causation produces far better results. p21

Thoughts are about possibilities. Possibilities are future events - which do not exist and may never exist. And yet possibilities influence the present. p21

This is a reversal of causal knowledge: order developing from disorder. How can we account for this?
