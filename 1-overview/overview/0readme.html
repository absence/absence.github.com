Read Me

Preface

Deacon tells you: Ex nililo fit - Nothing from nothing. A difference that makes no difference makes no difference.

Does reading a book make a difference?

If you feel that a book means something, has import or could be useful then what should you do?

If you just put a book that you have finished reading on the shelf and go on to the next book, then were you merely passing some of the time until you die?

This effort on a repository - the result of reading a book - is certainly also a way of passing time until death occurs.

Is there a significant difference between to two actions?

Only time will tell if the ongoing performances or unfurlings of Deacon's thoughts - as they pass into the minds of reader after reader - will a cause a significant difference.

And if the book causes a difference what would it be?

The repository allows you to re-read the book without re-reading the whole book. Like cue cards. Sign posts?

It's an aide memoire, an exigesis.

Secondly this repo tries to identify and highlight analytical tools that have been identified or are proposed.

This repo does not attempt to invent. The idea is to look for readily available giants that have backs to stand on.

Ontologies that are being used include:

Books: the traditional format of a book: table of contents, contents, index of contents.

Presentations: "Tell'em whatya goin to tell'em, Tell'em, then tell'em whatya told'em."

Newspapers: The five W's: Who, what when, where and why.

Programming: Differentiating between properties and methods, content and methods

Gaming: Gathering of assets while progressing through levels.

This repository does several things.

the first is a 'table of contents' written in an old fashioned way. [I have had a brief look through Google Books but have not yet found a good example of what I think I am looking for.]

In this sort of 'table' of contents' Every section is listed and each comes with a short description of the section.

Also books might have a summary at the beginning of each chapter.

The second thing this repo provides is an expanded index.

The third is an expanded glossary.

Assumptions

The first assumption is that you have read Incomplete Nature and are in the process of re-reading the book, trying to piece together the many topics that are covered while trying to retain what you have already read.

A second assumption is that you are juggling the book or ereader and have this document open on your computer.

Very quick access to secondary sources for every key topic mentioned in a section - almost entirely links to the relevant Wikipedia page

  • A highly annotated table of contents
  • Book summary
  • Part summaries
  • Chapter summaries
  • Section summaries
  • Text summaries

Notes regarding the Chapter Summaries

Key words are listed:

  • Chapter by chapter, section by section
  • In order of appearance
  • With first use in a chapter linked to relevant Wikipedia entry or relevant 'Explanations' entry
  • With second use of a person's name being last name only
  • Re-started each chapter
