Chapter 4 ~ Teleonomy

Teleology is like a mistress to a biologist: he cannot live without her but he’s unwilling to be seen with her in public. —J B S Haldane

A Teleology: is any philosophical account that holds that final causes exist in nature, meaning that design and purpose analogous to that found in human actions are inherent also in the rest of nature.

Colin Pittendrigh/1958: Teleonomy: law-like behavior, asymmetric, predictive.

1 Back to the future - p107

Key words: Entropy, Rudolf Clausius, Second law of thermodynamics, Time reversal [AKA T-symmetry] Aristotle, Final cause

This section: living things seem to violate the laws of nature: entropy rules. But It;s not really a law. And what's causing this?

Rudolf Clausius/1865: Entropy - the tendency in nature for things to fall apart.

Second law of thermodynamics: all other things being equal: entropy will go for broke.

Thus: Life and thinking - order and organization - are quite unnatural/appear to violate a universal law.

Loophole: Second law is a tendency - but not a necessity. There's room to wiggle here.

In life: Time reversal appears implicit as is Aristotle's 'final cause'.

What's the cause of this?

2 The Law of Effect - p109

Key words: Functions, Daniel Dennett, Darwin's Dangerous Idea, Charles Darwin, The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex Natural selection, Jean-Baptiste Lamarck, Edward Thorndike, Law of effect, Empedocles, Aristotle, Entelechy, Thomas Malthus, Jacques Monod, Chance and Necessity

This section: As part of the process of natural selection, organisms adapt: Law of effect. Replaces teleology

We know of functions as part of the process of design. Darwin taught us that functions may emerge spontaneously.

Daniel Dennet: logic of natural selection defeats claims that teleogy is necessary to explain complexity of biology

Theory of natural selection = statistics

Darwin: organisms - 1. reproduce, 2, diverge, 3. produce surfeit. Process generating inequalities. Differs with Lamarck's acquired characteristics.

Darwin's discovery/Descent of Man: logic of natural selection is after-the-fact. Favorable outcomes preferentially reproduced, retained or expressed. How these are generated matters not.

Edward Thorndike's Law of effect: organisms adapt their behavior to their environment.

Why did it take so long to discover? Humans are designers. Easier to think there's a supreme designer than to intuit the spontaneity.

Empedocles: orderliness may arrive spontaneously - by preserving accidents. Only combinations that produce balance/fitness likely to persist.

Aristotle disses Empedocles. How could the order and beauty we see be merely accidental? There's an entelechy lurking.

Darwin's thoughts: provoked by Malthus, supervene Larmarck. Mechanisms for reproducing and preserving are entirely separable.

Jacques Monod's Chance and Necessity: systems of molecular biology may be explained without final causality.

3 Pseudopurpose - p113

Key words: Ernst Mayr, Willar Van Ormand Quine, Colin Pittendrigh, Teleonomy, Gregory Bateson, Old Faithful, Walter Cannon

This section: life is more than a machine - needs a term of expressionL Teleonomy. Implies law-like behavior. Teleological in name only. A psudopurpose. This begs Teleomatic. But how closely do these terms map to reality?

Machines require assembly. Organisms develop spontaneously. Though they appear similar, they are opposites.

Ernst Mayer/1974: we should avoid confusing the two. Nevertheless both can be understood as thoroughly mechanistic - an exception: life itself.

p>Van Quine: Darwin shows that nature needs no final cause - she can be explained by physics and chemistry.

Colin Pittendrigh/1958: teleonomy: law-like behavior, asymmetric, predictive.

A mechanical process can be organized so it converges towards a specific state. Note: This is NOT behavior for the the sake of the end state.

Teleonomy: target-directed, but behavior agnostic, consequence-blind, accident-driven

Teleonomy: itself an accident, a mechanism.

Example: the thermostat (in a Bateson-like manner): it behaves precisely how a purpose-driven human would behave.

Example: Old Faithful: shows a teleonomic mechanism can arise by accident.

Thus: it/we/them may all be an accident.

Now consider human body temperature regulation: similar to thermostat, maintains a temperature.

But body temperature is not just any arbitrary temperature, it is the most suitable temperature for a variety of metabolic processes to operate optimally

Mayr: 'A teleonomic process or behavior is one which owes its goal-directedness to the operation of the program.'

BTW: But do not confuse an extrinsic abstract description with something that is intrinsic.

Mayr: Teleomatic: end-directed, in a passive automatic way, regulated by external forces or conditions.

BTW: Teleology, teleonomic, teleomatic: now widely accepted.

BTW: But which is the cause: human design, natural selection or happy accident?

Many adaptive features, embedded in an organism, exist because of the contribution to something else.

Is a teleonomic mechanism that helps preserve a larger system to persist and reproduce the same as a mechanism generated by accident?

4 Blood, brains, and silicon - p124

Key words: Humoral immune response, Hypermutation, Blanks slate [aka Tabula rasa], , B F Skinner, Karl Popper, Evolutionary epistemology, Donald T Campbell, Blind variation and selective retention, Gerald Edelman< Immunoglobulin [aka antibody], Artificial life

This section outlines various areas where natural selection has been seen to replace teleology.

A success for Law of Effect: Humoral immune response.

Common sense suggested immune response was a blank slate - ready to accept what was presented to it

reality: in pre-natal state hypermutation produces vast array of forms. Later, when exposed to disease, with a bit of luck some of these forms bind with the enemy. If it fits, this is the signal to make more of the same.

Most will never bu used. Fit is only approximate. No information is acquired.

B F Skinner and behaviorism: spontaneous exploratory behavior analogous to genetic variation. Reward or punishment analogous to selection pressures. Habit formation or extinction analogous to differential reproduction

Karl Popper's evolutionary epistemology; progressive adaptation of scientific knowledge to its empirical subject matter.

Donald T Campbell's blind variation and selective retention.

Gerald Edelman: start with an overabundance of neurons and connections. Keep the ones that best reflect converging signal correlations.

Artifical life: computer simulation of natural selection or unnatural selection. Teleogy free - though parameters are extrinsically determined. But remember it's only a game.

5 Fractions of life - p128

Key words: DNA, RNA, Ernst Mayr, Richard Dawkins, Selfish Gene, Turing Machine, Jesper Hoffmeyer, Biosemiotics, Claus Emmeche, Prions, Paul Weiss, Élan vital

This section is about replication and natural selection at the molecular level. Dawkins provides a reasonable caricature of genes(software) and organisms(hardware) as an information processing system.

But an orgamism is not a assembly of parts. An organism grows. Different kettle of fish. Thus Dawkin's and other people's analysis - based on machine paradigms - are are either over-simplifications or incomplete or smuggle in patterns - thus creating numerous homunculi.

DNA and RNA mean that life may be understood in the terns of information processing. 'Algorithms appears to be a reasonable caricature.

Even evolution may be explained in these terms with he introduction of errors into the code.

Richard Dawkins's Selfish Gene (1976): possible to think of evolution in informational terms - with genes as the organiational software

But DNA (software) needs an organism (hardware) to run in. For Dawkins; the organism is the medium through which genes influence their probability of being replicated

Jesper Hoffmeyer: selection external/mutations internal. But mutation is random

Claus Emmech: organism becomes passive meeting place for forces alien to itself.

Prions: good example of the complexity of self-replication

Dawkins is not incorrect, but equating information with pattern smuggles in systemic properties

Paul Weiss (1960s): Biology builds prosthetic devices to make up for amputations to the organic wholeness.

Organisms are not mere assembles of parts. They grow.

Élan vital hasn't been snuffed, it's just been pushed into smaller parts within parts - tiny simpler bits have to do the heavy lifting.
